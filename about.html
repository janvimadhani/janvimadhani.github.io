About Rodrigo

I am a fifth year Astronomy and Astrobiology graduate student at the University of Washington in Seattle. My research interests include the detection and characterization of exoplanets and investigations into their habitability. I focus primarily on planets orbiting low-mass M dwarfs, stars whose vigorous magnetic activity and evolutionary history pose interesting challenges to the habitability of their planets. As of this past year, I have also been trying to detect planets in the habitable zones of these low mass stars with the K2 mission. Check out my research projects below for more information.
